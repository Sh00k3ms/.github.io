🕵️ Under the Hood CTF: How to Hack the Stack (Without Losing Your Mind)

Welcome, hacker. If you’ve found yourself here, odds are you’ve either:

  • Been handed a strange-looking sticker with a QR code,
  • Clicked something you maybe weren’t supposed to,
  • Or you're trying to track down the elusive Grifter.

Either way? Respect.

This writeup walks through the logic behind each phase of the Under the Hood CTF, but won’t spoil it all outright. Think of it more like a hacker's field journal — with nudges, hints, and a bit of Defcon lore.

Phase 1: Cipher Warmup 🔓

You’re greeted with a classic cipher challenge. If you’ve got even half a cryptographer’s brain, you’ll recognize what kind of shift is going on here. Think ROT, think Caesar. Easy start — don’t overthink it.

🧠 Hint: If A = D, what happened?

Phase 2: Headers Up 🧠

Here you’ll need to inspect what the server is really saying — not just what the browser displays. It’s hiding in plain sight.

🧠 Hint: Dev tools are your best friend. Check the headers.

Phase 3: Symbol of the Hackers ⚙️

This is about hacker culture. Specifically, a symbol introduced in 2003 by someone with strong opinions about open source.

🧠 Hint: Game of Life + ESR + 5 cells = ?

Phase 4: Don’t Get Nabbed 💀

You’ve seen this kind of attack before, even if you didn’t know what to call it. Switch tabs, and suddenly the login page isn’t what it was. Feels fishy.

🧠 Hint: You got nabbed. But what kind?

Phase 5: The Grifter Hunt 👒

And now… the final phase. You’ve proven your skills, but now comes the real challenge:

Find Grifter. In real life.

The Lore:

Grifter is a legendary figure at DEF CON. Hard to find. Rarely stays still. That’s why his underlings (Contest Goon crew) decided to track him. Not to interfere — just to observe. You’ll need to ask the right goons for a tip on where he might be next.

What You’ll Need:

  • A working knowledge of the CTF name.
  • Social engineering skills (lightweight — don’t be creepy).
  • Confidence to ask a Contest Goon, “Any word on Grifter’s location?”

If you find him? You’ll get a QR code.

There are two:

  • 🏆 Winner QR: For the first to find him.
  • 🐾 Solved QR: For everyone else who still makes it.

Both will log your handle — so choose it wisely.

This CTF wasn’t about brute force. It was about:

  • Paying attention.
  • Knowing your tools.
  • Understanding hacker culture.
  • Asking for help at the right time.

If you made it through all five phases and found Grifter in the wild? You’re part of the pack now.

Respect the stack. Respect the pack.
